In Sensible Reviews

Qaidi Band

Vicky Sivakumar



SS rating : 3/5

SS verdict: Asking the right questions but failing to answer them

The rhetoric that music can elevate people in dire circumstances seemed unreal but the real success of this film was to keep it realistic. The dreary tone of the film portraying jail life for diverse people connects strongly. This movie feels like a Bollywood version of ‘Sense8’ with its diverse representation. This makes sure that this film feels unique as well as relatable to any type of audience.

The movie begins well and makes us excited as a song made by these convicts go viral. Maybe retribution is at hand and gives us hope but it is quickly snuffed out. The first half is a roller coaster of emotions as the protagonists face both faces of life – light and the dark ones. The emotions we go through makes us question certain norms in society and we eagerly wait for the answers and their plight in the second half.

The movie which struck a chord with the audience in the first half fails to do so in the second half. The answers we expect are too simple to be practical. This is the point the movie becomes unreal and succumbs to the happy ending cliche. Yes, nobody wants them to not make it in the end but not at the cost of the story. The relatibility and empathy the movie earned just dwindles off.

Though the movie showed promise, it failed to carry forward the story and the message simultaneously which Udta Punjab successfully did. Yet, the intriguing questions and the diverse connect makes this movie worth a watch.
