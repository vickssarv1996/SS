In Sensible Reviews

Vivegam

Threka



SS rating : 2/5

SS verdict: Spoof of an Action Film

Yazhini (Kajal) who is pregnant is trapped in a house with assassins trying to kill her. AK (Ajith) presumably understands the morse code she taps on the floor and is probably able to pinpoint the assassins’ locations using it. He then assassinates them with precision using a sniper gun. At one point in the sequence, her back is turned to him. Unable to communicate, he whispers “Move to the right” to her who is out of hearing range by at least 500m. Lo and Behold! She moves in the nick of time for him to take out another goon. There is a pause, almost like the characters are waiting for something to resume their roles.

In the past few sentences, I had to use so many adjectives for uncertainty like presumably and probably. That is because, throughout the film, the director asks us to take quantum leaps of faith for each frame to have a mildly entertaining experience. (Only Ajith hardcore fans seem to have this skill) Otherwise the whole narrative falls to pieces if watched with a logically sound mind. The agonising part from all of this is that Siva seems to have taken a leap of faith himself in scenes where he expected the audience to cheer. The pause I mentioned in the scene above is one such instance where he gives a time allowance for raucous cheering to make sure the audience’s experience is not disrupted. But at each of these instances, the scenes fail to elicit any emotions as it is greeted only by awkward silences. And when the characters wait for appreciation on screen, the absence of it from the audience is painful and pitiful to watch.

Vetri has done a fairly good job with the visuals and Anirudh delivers his usual stuff. Vetri’s work is surprising and is the only high point. Ajith seems to have done a lot of hard work for this (The film tirelessly pushed that down our throats) but Siva needs to understand that that is not enough to carry the movie. Yes, the action sequences were visually good but the movie tries so hard to be an action packed blockbuster that at one point, it is just funny. The amount of gizmos and ammunition just short-circuits our brains to the point where we become numb to it. Every detail and twist seemed forced and the superhuman description of the protagonist also doesn’t help.

Collapsing on its cliches and twists, Vivegam’s creators should have had some ‘vivegam’ to not waste the hard work and perseverance of a crowd pulling star on a careless plot. Similarly, as an actor, Ajith needs to responsibly choose his films as like it or not, he does have a huge fan base. “With great power comes great responsibility”.
