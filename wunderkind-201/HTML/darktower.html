In Sensible Reviews

The Dark Tower

Vicky Sivakumar



SS rating :2.25/5

SS verdict: Maybe books are better off themselves

A wounded Idris Elba (Gunslinger) hides behind a pillar for cover as almost 100 shooters gather against him. The pillar is almost obliterated with the amount of incoming bullets as tensions rise. He reloads his gun and takes a deep breath. And at that point, I knew that the creators did not care. He just jumps into the action and in minutes, all the 100 shooters are obliterated. How? Just plain shooting with 2 Excalibur revolvers. It has come to the point that Hollywood no longer has the license to mock other industries for comprimising in logic.

After watching the movie, the genre is confusing. Fantasy, sure. All the centre of the universe, seers and towers stuff made sure of that. (Though nobody cared to explain about them and it frankly didn’t make much sense) But surely it can’t be action. More like a badly executed drama. With such a thin storyline, the only way for this to work would have been great action. You get 10 minutes of action, period. (5 minutes of 100 men shooting shit) Even the small problems which initially sparks our interest have very lame solutions like Gunslinger shooting with just his hearing sense.

Both Elba and McConaughey are so underused that you can barely see a sliver of their talent. Mathew McConaughey is a poor shadow of David Tennant’s Kilgrave in Jessica Jones. He does things with his mind bending sorcery but the director didn’t seem to have the stomach or imagination to show its full potential unlike Kilgrave. This stellar cast itself was not able to hold this ship steady ‘cos the story was riddled with too many holes.

Overall, a very mediocre effort on a bestselling book. Intrigue is always the secret to hook the audience into fantasy but nothing stood out in the movie to capture the attention of the audience. The Dark tower badly needs something darker for the franchise to stay alive.
