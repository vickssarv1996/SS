In Insight

Between The Lines

Vicky Sivakumar



Cineplay, the new attempt at storytelling by Hotstar brings forth its next production, ‘Between the Lines’. Featuring prominent faces like Nandita Das and Subodh Maskara, the feature seemed a promising one. Unlike the other Cineplay productions which are cinematic adaptations of contemporary plays written by stalwarts like Dr. Vijay Tendulkar, this one seems to have been co-written and adapted by Nandita Das herself. An urban take on feminism and feministic sentiments, Nandita Das effortlessly takes the center stage. The total number of cast members is exactly two. Yes, just the 2 protagonists. Hence, this 75 minute feature has to tackle the daunting task of not boring the audience with the same faces.

From the outset, the set and the tone feel artificial. Takes some time to adjust. The reality slips as we are not able to accept the fact that they speak English at home. Either English is not so deeply rooted in our country as we believe or the actors have very bad accents. Either way, it takes us some time to get accustomed to. But once we set aside those, we find that the story tries to tackle a chronic problem, in India especially. Though Shekar (Subodh) constantly talks about showing no emotions, the story hinges solely on emotion. Shekar and Maya (Nandita) are a lawyer couple and predictably find themselves opposite each other in a criminal case. Though the story promises a case, the story is bland with no ups and downs. For the first time in the history of cinema, it is actually an open and shut case. The clients are an embodiment of the raw masculine in Shekar and the raw feminine in Maya. To drive that point home, both the lawyer and the client are portrayed by the same person.

Nandita carries the whole feature on her own as somehow she captures our attention over this slow melodrama. Her subtle expressions and dialogue delivery further attests her validity as an artist. Subodh struggles to keep up with her but on screen, they make a nice pair. The urban woman’s dilemma on whether she is really empowered or relatively more empowered is the central question behind the whole plot. Though the actors provide a deep and meaningful rendition of the script, it is not an exaggeration to say that the writing is amateurish as the drama flows into melodrama most of the time.

The feature does have its flaws but Nandita pulls off an amazing performance as a confused urban woman who understands true freedom while fighting for her client’s. Theater is dwindling all over the country, especially in the southern circuit. Cineplay is a laudable effort to revive theater culture and should be supported. ‘Between the lines’ is a commendable effort and a lesson for all artists on the importance of acting. Like the story, Nandita comes out victorious!
